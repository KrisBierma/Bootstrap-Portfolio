Building things

I’m a web developer who has always liked logic games, writing analytical papers, putting together jigsaw puzzles, and creating projects for my students. I love asking questions and am an information junkie. In a way, coding takes a little bit of all these and mashes it together into a career fit for my personality. It’s precise, organized, puzzling, and it stretches the little grey cells to the limit.

My undergraduate degree in English - Creative Writing taught me to hone my writing and analyzing skills. The master’s in education and ten years of teaching taught me to analyze data to influence how I taught. I am used to breaking things down into manageable, well-thought-out steps, and I know how to think ahead to possible questions or problems which may arise. A University of Texas intensive coding bootcamp helped me hone front- and back-end technology skills and allowed me the opportunity to collaborate with other coders.

Over the years I’ve created, built, or produced many things, including literature guides, bathroom cabinets (I love power tools), and orchestrating twenty-two third graders’ individualized programs of study on iPads. Now I build other, slightly less tactile, things like My Veggie Eats, a useful app for finding vegetarian and vegetarian-friendly restaurants, and games like Science Trivia and Hangman, Colors Edition.
